ISO 31000:2018 — Clause 6.4.3 Risk analysis
Comprehend the nature of risk and its characteristics including level of risk (Cl. 6.4.3). Analysis considers: likelihood of events and consequences, nature and magnitude of consequences, control effectiveness, complexity and connectivity, time-related factors, sensitivity and confidence levels. Analysis may be qualitative, quantitative or a combination.

ISO 31000:2018 — Clause 6.4.4 Risk evaluation
Compare risk analysis results with risk criteria to determine where action is required (Cl. 6.4.4). Evaluation outcomes: do nothing further · consider treatment options · undertake further analysis · maintain existing controls · reconsider objectives. Decisions should account for wider context and actual/perceived consequences to stakeholders.

ISO 31000:2018 — Clause 6.5 Risk treatment
Select and implement options for addressing risk (Cl. 6.5.1–6.5.3). Treatment is an iterative process: formulate options → select → plan → implement → assess effectiveness → decide if residual risk is acceptable → if not, take further treatment. Treatment can introduce new risks that must themselves be managed.
